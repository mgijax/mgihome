The Mouse Gene Expression Information Resource Project

The Mouse Gene Expression Information Resource (MGEIR) is being developed collaboratively by the Gene Expression Database (GXD) project and the Edinburgh Mouse Atlas project. Ultimately, the MGEIR will provide the scientific community with a unified resource that combines text-based and 3D graphical methods to store, display, and analyze mouse developmental gene expression information.

The Mouse Gene Expression Information Resource resource will integrate the following components:

  • Gene Expression Database (GXD)
    Integrates different types of expression data and provides links to many other resources to place the data into the larger biological and analytical context. The time and location of gene expression is described using the Dictionary of Anatomical Terms, which is part of the Anatomy Database. Database records in GXD are complemented using 2D images of the original expression data. (For further details, see About GXD).

  • Anatomy Database
    Provides the standard nomenclature for developmental anatomy. The dictionary names the tissues and structures for each developmental stage. A thesaurus of alternative terminology adds flexibility to database queries. Fate tables listing the progenitors and derivatives for each structure will enable analysis of differentiation pathways. You can browse the anatomy and find gene expression data for structures with the Mouse Developmental Anatomy Browser.

  • 3D Atlas / Graphical Gene Expression Database
    Provides a high-resolution digital representation of mouse anatomy reconstructed from serial sections of single embryos at each representative developmental stage enabling 3D graphical display and analysis of in situ expression data. The tissues of the 3D atlas are labeled and assigned to the terms from the anatomical dictionary. These assignments provide links to the Gene Expression Database. In the intermediate term, in situ expression patterns observed can be colored-in into the 3D reconstructions. The ultimate goal is to transfer electronically digitized in situ data from different embryos into the 3D atlas.

Current Status and Acknowledgements

GXD has been developed and continues to be maintained and enhanced by the GXD team at the Jackson Laboratory.

The Dictionary of Anatomical Terms for structures present during embryonic development (Theiler Stages 1 - 26) has been developed and continues to be refined at the Department of Biomedical Sciences, University of Edinburgh, Scotland and the MRC Human Genetics Unit, Edinburgh. A dictionary of anatomical terms for the postnatal mouse (Theiler Stage 28) has been developed and is currently being refined by the GXD project. It is available for viewing here.

The 3D Atlas / Graphical Gene Expression Database is being developed at the MRC Human Genetics Unit, Edinburgh and at the Department of Biomedical Sciences, University of Edinburgh, Scotland.

The GXD Project is supported by NIH grant HD062499. Work at Edinburgh is supported by the MRC, the BBSRC, and by the European Science Foundation.
