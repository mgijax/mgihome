About GXD

The purpose of the Gene Expression Database (GXD) is to store and integrate the many types of gene expression data on the laboratory mouse; make these data freely and widely available in formats appropriate for thorough analysis; and provide links to other relevant resources placing this expression information within a larger biological and analytical context.

Currently, the following data are searchable:

  • GXD Index, a searchable index of research reports documenting data on endogeneous gene expression during mouse development, including references, genes and embryonic stages analyzed, and expression assays used.

  • Mouse cDNA data, including I.M.A.G.E. clones and ESTs.

  • RT-PCR, RNA in situ hybridization, Immunohistochemistry, Northern and Western Blot data

GXD includes a large data set of RT-PCR data reporting expression data for 517 genes in 46 tissues (provided by Tom Freeman et al,The Sanger Centre, UK). Additional expression data from the various assay types are now being entered into the database and made publicly available on a daily basis.

Currently, these data are acquired from the literature by database editors. In the future, we will acquire data primarily via electronic submission from laboratories. Prototypes of an electronic submission system are being tested and refined in collaboration with a number of laboratories in North America and Europe with the aim to produce a robust submission system for the community at large.
