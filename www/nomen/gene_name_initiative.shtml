The Mouse Genome Nomenclature group is evaluating gene names and symbols for mouse protein coding genes with gene model nomenclature to make them more informative. New names and symbols are based on annotations of protein domains from InterPro. Name changes are coordinated with the Human Gene Nomenclature Committee, Rat Genome Database and with the GenCode mouse genome annotation team. Contact nomen@jax.org with suggestions or comments regarding the new names.

What's new in mouse gene nomenclature?
Histone genes have been renamed at the request of histone researchers. We renamed the genes where the histone experts agree that mouse and human genes are orthologous (experts such as Paul Talbert and David Landsman; a paper will be published in the near future). This resulted in the streamlining of histone nomenclature. For example, Hist1h2aa has become H2ac1 in parallel to its human ortholog H2AC1; H2afy is now Macroh2a1, human is MACROH2A1, in keeping with the wishes of histone researchers.
