8 Basic rules for naming mouse loci

Mouse locus symbols...

  1. are short, 3-4 characters recommended as acronym for the locus name, but not to exceed 10 characters.

  2. begin with a letter and contain only alphanumeric characters.

  3. are similar for members of a gene family, use a common stem followed by 1-2 alphanumerics.

  4. contain no punctuation; hyphens are permitted when they separate two adjacent numbers (e.g. Lamb1-2).

  5. do contain hyphens in related sequence and pseudogene symbols (e.g. Adh5-ps1).

  6. begin with a lower case letter if the locus is a newly discovered recessive mutation (e.g. wi); in all other cases, they begin with a capital letter.

  7. anonymous DNA loci follow the convention D#XXX%e where # = chromosome location; XXX = lab code and % = serial number in series. A trailing "e" is added only when the DNA segment is known to be expressed. The current list of approved lab codes are available from ILAR (Institute of Laboratory Animal Resources, Washington, D.C.)

  8. are italicized in published articles
For more detail and additional special case rules, see Mouse Nomenclature Rules and Guidelines.

Checklist for Proposing a Locus Symbol

  1. Determine that the phenotype is genetic:
    • Inherited in a Mendelian fashion
    • Cloned locus sequence

  2. Search searches/marker_form.shtml">MGD and GDB to ensure the entry does not already exist.

  3. Refer to the Mouse Nomenclature Rules and Guidelines to formulate possible combinations of letters for the symbol.

  4. Check searches/marker_form.shtml">MGD and GDB to see which combinations from (3) are available.

  5. Check ordered lists of mouse and human symbols to ensure that the proposed new symbol will not interrupt an existing "family" of locus symbols.

  6. Check that the proposed symbol is not in common use in other contexts such as mouse and human locus mapping literature:
    • Searches of Entrez, SwissProt or Medline can help to identify such problems

  7. Check similar entries in searches/marker_form.shtml">MGD and GDB to ensure that the proposed symbol is constructed in the same manner as related symbols..
