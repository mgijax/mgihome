Checklist for Proposing a Locus Symbol  
  1. Determine that the phenotype is genetic:
    • Inherited in a Mendelian fashion
    • Cloned locus sequence

  2. Search MGD, RGD, and HGNC to ensure that the entry does not already exist.

  3. Refer to the Rules for Nomenclature of Genes, Genetic Markers, Alleles, and Mutations in Mouse and Rat to formulate possible combinations of letters for the symbol.

  4. Check MGD, RGD, and HGNC to see which combinations from (3) are available.

  5. Check ordered lists of mouse and human symbols to ensure that the proposed new symbol will not interrupt an existing "family" of locus symbols.

  6. Check that the proposed symbol is not in common use in other contexts such as mouse and human locus mapping literature:
    • Searches of Entrez, SWISS-PROT or PubMed can help to identify such problems.

  7. Check similar entries in MGD, RGD, and HGNC to ensure that the proposed symbol is constructed in the same manner as related symbols.
