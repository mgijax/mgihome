Superfamily Note:  Cylic nucleotides (e.g., cGMP; guanosine-3',5'-cyclic monophosphate) mediate cellular responses to external signals as secondary messengers. The concentration of cyclic nucleotides is in turn controlled by the cyclic nucleotide phosphodiesterases (Pde#). Pde# have been shown to be a large superfamily of diverse enzymes that are highly regulated by multiple signaling pathways.

Notes About Gene Family Annotations in MGD

Nomenclature for New Genes

Contact the MGD Nomenclature Committee (nomen@informatics.jax.org) for approved gene symbols prior to submission. This avoids any possible conflicts with existing symbols and ensures that the gene symbol and name are reserved in the MGD private nomenclature database. All information is kept confidential until publication.

Nomenclature for Gene Families

MGD works closely with HUGO and other interested scientists in the establishment of nomenclature for genes that are members of gene families and in representing the relationships between members of gene families. In general, a stem symbol is assigned (e.g., Entpd) and members of the family are numbered (e.g., Entpd1, Entpd2, etc.). However, there are exceptions and many special cases.
