Subj.: Genes and Mutations - Symbol Changes

When spontaneous mouse mutant genes are cloned, many of them have two symbols: one for the structural locus and one for the mutation. The International Committee on Standardized Genetic Nomenclature for Mice has approved the revision of mutant gene symbols to show that they are mutant alleles of the structural gene. The simple rule is that, when a spontaneous mutation is cloned or shown to occur in a previously named candidate gene, the mutation's symbol is changed to become an allele at the cloned locus by turning the mutation symbol into an allele symbol, e.g. the shi (shiverer) mutation in the Mbp (myelin basic protein) gene becomes Mbpshi.

The symbol changes are being made now in the Mouse Genome Database (MGD), the TJL strain registry, and the 1995-96 price list. Attached are a list of mutations that are being changed and the rules governing such changes. Note: the albino gene has been exempted from the change - at least for now - because of its historical significance.
