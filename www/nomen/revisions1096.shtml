Nomenclature Rules Revisions - October 1996

The International Committee approved the rules changes shown below at its meeting in Paris 7 October 1996:

Revised rules - Gene Nomenclature

1.1.1. Add: Hyphens shall be deleted from gene names as well as symbols.

1.1.2.9. Expressed sequence tagged (EST) loci, when mapped to chromosomes, may be given either D symbols with a final e for expressed or the symbol ESTM###, where M identifies mouse as opposed to human EST loci.and ### = a serial number assigned from the Mouse Genome Database (MGD).

New rule - Gene Nomenclature::

1.1.7.7a. When an existing gene is replaced with a different, functional gene, called a "knock-in," the symbol shall be written as an allele of the original gene. For example, if Myf5 is deleted and replaced with Myod, Myf5Myod-labcode# when there is a more-or-less complete knock-in, or by Myf5m-labcode# when it is a domain swap of some kind. The domain(s) swapped should be indicated by letters such as P=promotor, E=enhancing element, E#, = exon #; e.g. Myf5mP-labcode#. The labcode is an investigator or institution's Laboratory Registration Code (used also for strain holders, DNA symbols, and targeted mutations) and the # is a serial number for that laboratory.

New rule - Strain nomenclature

3.6. Advanced Intercross Lines (AIL) are made by producing an F2 generation between two inbred strains and then, in each subsequent generation, intercrossing mice but avoiding sibling matings. The purpose is to increase the possibility of tightly linked genes recombining. Symbols shall combine outbred stock and mixed inbred strain nomenclature with the generation number included in the symbol to distinguish these stocks from mixed inbred strains. Generations shall be designated G3, G4, etc. beginning with the first non-sib cross after the F2 generation. E.g. The stock Pri:B6,D2-G# is an AIL created at Princeton from the inbred strains C57BL/6 x DBA/2. The G number will increase with each generation.

Reminder: There are two ways in which mode of inheritance of mutant genes is conveyed in the gene symbol:

Inheritance of spontaneous mutations is indicated by the first letter of the gene symbol; inheritance of all other genes (cloned genes, targeted mutations, etc.) is indicated in the allele symbol. The rule is #1.1.7.2 in the Rules and Guidelines (available at the URL address http://www.informatics.jax.org/support/submissions/nomen/).
