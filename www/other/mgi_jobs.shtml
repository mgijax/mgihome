Bioinformatics Analyst

The Mouse Genome Informatics Group has an opening for a Bioinformatics Analyst. Our group develops biological databases and software tools for scientists and medical researchers worldwide who use the laboratory mouse to study genetics and human diseases. Areas of coverage include genes and gene function, expression, mutations, phenotypes, sequence, SNPs and other polymorphisms, strains, and mammalian homology. (http://www.informatics.jax.org/). As a key member of our software development team, the Bioinformatics Analyst leads the requirements analysis for new features added to our information system, bridging the gap between our scientific users and our software engineering staff. Applicants must have an M.S. or Ph.D. in biology or a related field and demonstrable computational skills, or a B.S. in computer science with 5+ years of software development and requirements analysis experience in a bioinformatics domain. Desired skills include: Web site design, HTML, relational databases, SQL, scripting/programming experience, knowledge of public bioinformatics resources (NCBI, EMBL-EBI, SWISS-PROT, Ensembl, and so on), experience with sequence analysis applications (BLAST, BLAT, GCG, and so on), and Unix. Excellent written and oral communication skills required.

Scientific Curators

Professional positions are available as Scientific Curators in the expanding Mouse Genome Informatics Programs at The Jackson Laboratory working with the Mouse Genome Database, Gene Expression Database, Mouse Tumor Biology Database, and Rat Genome Database Collaboration. Successful candidates will be primarily responsible for data acquisition and analysis, evaluating and annotating data to be incorporated into the databases, integrating information from disparate sources, and interacting with research laboratories and genome centers to facilitate data transfer. In addition, Curators take part in database and interface design by contributing biological perspectives to new data content and displays. Specific areas of preferred expertise are detailed below for available positions. Other desirable attributes include excellent writing / communication skills and the ability to work effectively in a team environment. Ph.D. degree in Life Sciences or M.S. with appropriate experience required.

  • Mouse Genome Database (MGD) Project - Applicants with strong backgrounds in mammalian genetics and comparative genomics or in the use of mouse mutants as models of human disease preferred. MGD integrates a wide range mouse of genetic, genomic, and phenotypic data including gene characterization, gene localization and sequence, homologies and comparative mapping among mammals, phenotype characteristics, allelic variants and mutants, and strain data.
    Current needs are for individuals with mouse genetics, immunology, development, or phenotyping backgrounds. Experience and/or knowledge of genetic engineering techniques in mouse preferred.

  • Gene Expression Database (GXD) Project - Applicants with strong backgrounds in molecular and developmental biology and anatomy preferred. GXD integrates different types of gene expression information from the laboratory mouse and places them in the larger biological and analytical context via integration with MGD and by curating links to many other resources.
    Current needs are for individuals with microarray expertise as well as other high-throughput methodologies such as SAGE and MPSS.

  • Mouse Tumor Biology Database (MTB) Project - Applicants with strong backgrounds in tumor biology, cancer genetics, and mouse models preferred. MTB integrates data on frequency, incidence, genetics, and pathology of neoplastic disorders, emphasizing data on tumors that develop characteristically in different genetically defined strains of mice.

  • Rat Genome Database (RGD) Collaboration - Applicants with strong backgrounds in mammalian genetics and comparative genomics preferred. RGD is a genomics database developed at the Medical College of Wisconsin to support the rat as a model organism. Our role in this collaboration includes establishing new rat gene nomenclature and curating gene orthology data for rat, mouse, and human.
