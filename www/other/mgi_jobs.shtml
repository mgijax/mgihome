Scientific Curators

Professional positions are available as Scientific Curators in the Mouse Genome Informatics programs at The Jackson Laboratory working with the Mouse Genome Database, the Mouse Tumor Biology Database, and the Knockout Mouse Project Database. Successful candidates will be primarily responsible for data acquisition and analysis, evaluating and annotating data to be incorporated into the databases, integrating information from disparate sources, and interacting with research laboratories and genome centers to facilitate data transfer. In addition, curators take part in database and interface design by contributing biological perspectives to new data content and display. Specific areas of preferred expertise are detailed below for available positions. Other desirable attributes include excellent writing / communication skills and the ability to work effectively in a team environment. Ph.D. degree in Life Sciences required; exceptional individuals with M.S. degrees and extensive experience will be considered. A cover letter should be submitted with your resume.

Positions are currently open in the:

  • Mouse Genome Database (MGD) Project - Applicants should have a strong background in mammalian genetics, comparative genomics, or in the use of mouse mutants as models of human disease. Knowledge of genetic engineering techniques, immunology, disease-specific areas, and embryonic development preferred. MGD integrates a wide range of mouse genetic, genomic, and phenotypic data, including gene characterization, gene localization and sequence, homologies and comparative mapping among mammals, phenotype characteristics, allelic variants and mutants, and strain data.

  • Mouse Tumor Biology Database (MTB) Project - Applicants with strong backgrounds in tumor biology, cancer genetics, and mouse models preferred. MTB integrates data on frequency, incidence, genetics, and pathology of neoplastic disorders, emphasizing data on tumors that characteristically develop in different genetically-defined strains of mice.

Software Staff

The Mouse Genome Informatics Group (MGI) at The Jackson Laboratory has openings for experienced software engineers. Our group develops biological databases and software tools used worldwide by scientists and medical researchers using the laboratory mouse to study genetics and human diseases. (http://www.informatics.jax.org)

Positions are currently open for:

  • Scientific Software Engineer II/III

    • The Mouse Tumor Biology Database Group at The Jackson Laboratory has a full-time opening for a scientific software engineer. This individual will be responsible for developing, maintaining, and expanding the Mouse Tumor Biology Database (http://tumor.informatics.jax.org) that is used worldwide by scientists and medical researchers studying mouse models of human cancer. Applicants must be energetic, imaginative, and ready to participate in a highly interdisciplinary environment combining biology and computer science. We seek a senior or experienced intermediate-level software engineer possessing experience and familiarity with Unix, CVS, full software development lifecycle, web and application servers, Java programming and related frameworks (Struts, JSP, Servlets, J2EE), and relational database experience (Sybase). The applicant should have good organizational skills, excellent communication skills, and be comfortable working in a team environment. B.S. in Computer Science required. Strong interest in biology preferred.

The Jackson Laboratory

Mountains, ocean, forests, lakes, and trails are all within walking distance. If you love high-tech challenges but you're looking for a more natural environment, this could be the opportunity you've been searching for.
