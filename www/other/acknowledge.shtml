Acknowledgements

MGI provides links to an increasing number of databases on the WWW and many of these sites provide links to MGI as well. We wish to acknowledge these valuable scientific information resources.

ArkDB

Roslin Institute, Edinborough, Scotland. URL (for cat or horse data): http://www.ri.bbsrc.ac.uk/cbioinformatics/databases.html

ARK is currently used by the Pig, Chicken, Sheep, Cattle, Cat and Horse genome mapping communities. The ARK system is currently installed at Invermay, New Zealand as well as at the Roslin Institute where it was developed. There will soon be ARK mirror nodes in the USA.

ATCC

American Type Culture Collection, 12301 Parklawn Drive, Rockville, MD. URL: http://www.atcc.org/

The mission of the ATCC is to acquire, authenticate, and maintain reference cultures, related biological materials, and associated data, and to distribute these to qualified scientists in government, industry, and education.

BovMap

Institut National de Recherche Agronomique, Laboratoire de genetique biochimique - Jouy-en-Josas. URL: http://locus.jouy.inra.fr/cgi-bin/bovmap/intro2.pl?BASE=cattle

The BovMap database offers to the scientific community working on the bovine genome a useful and practical tool to follow the rapid progress of the mapping effort.

dbEST

NCBI - Database for Expressed Sequences Tags. URL: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/dbEST/index.html
dbEST is a division of GenBank that contains sequence data and other information on "single-pass" cDNA sequences, or Expressed Sequence Tags, from a number of organisms.

DDBJ

DNA Data Bank of Japan (DDBJ), National Institute of Genetics, Mishima, Japan. URL: http://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/.

DDBJ is the only DNA data bank based in Japan. Sequence information is received from Japanese researchers, researchers from other countries, and from other sequence databases, including EMBL and GenBank.

EMBL

EMBL Nucleotide Sequence Database, maintained by European Bioinformatics Institute Cambridge, United Kingdom. URL: http://www.ebi.ac.uk/ebi_docs/embl_db/ebi/topembl.html
The European Bioinformatics Institute (EBI) of the European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL) maintains a number of databases including the EMBL Nucleotide Sequence Database and the SWISS-PROT Protein Sequence Database.

GenBank

GenBank, produced and maintained by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI), the National Library of Medicine (NLM), National Institutes of Healthe (NIH), Bethesda, Maryland. URL: http://www3.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/Entrez/index.html

GenBank is an interface providing access to three databases : a subset of the National Library of Medicine's MEDLINE database, the NCBI protein database, and the NCBI nucleotide database.

GenBank is produced and maintained by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). NCBI is responsible for building, maintaining, and distributing GenBank, the NIH genetic sequence database that collects all known DNA sequences from scientists worldwide. The NCBI is a division of the National Library of Medicine (NLM) and is located on the campus of the National Institutes of Health (NIH) in Bethesda, Maryland.

ENZYME

ENZYME, the Enzyme nomenclature database, maintained at the ExPASy molecular biology server of the Geneva University Hospital and the University of Geneva, Switzerland. URL: http://www.expasy.ch/sprot/enzyme.html.
ENZYME is a repository of information relative to the nomenclature of enzymes. It is primarily based on the recommendations of the Nomenclature Committee of the International Union of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology (IUBMB) and it describes each type of characterized enzyme for which an EC (Enzyme Commission) number has been provided.

FlyBase

FlyBase is available on the web from various sites including Harvard University. URL: http://flybase.harvard.edu:7081/.
FlyBase is a comprehensive database for information on the genetics and molecular biology of Drosophila. It includes data from the Drosophila Genome Projects and data curated from the literature. FlyBase is a joint project with the Berkeley and European Drosophila Genome Projects.

Genome Database (GDB)

GDB(TM) Human Genome Data Base, Baltimore, Maryland, USA): Johns Hopkins University, 1990-. URL: http://gdbwww.gdb.org/gdb/
GDB contains human genomic mapping data submitted by researchers worldwide and provides this information to the scientific community.

GSDB

Genome Sequence Data Base (GSDB) produced by the National Center for Genome Resources, Santa Fe, New Mexico. URL: http://www.ncgr.org/gsdb/

The Genome Sequence Data Base (GSDB) is a collection of DNA sequence data and related information. GSDB is dedicated to supporting scientific research and development by creating, maintaining and distributing a complete, timely, accurate and useful collection of DNA sequences and related information. As an on-line, client-server, relational database, GSDB operates as part of the DOE federated information infrastructure and focuses on meeting the needs of the major genome sequencing laboratories.

OMIM

Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man, OMIM (TM). The National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI), National Institutes of Health, Bethesda, Maryland. URL: http://www3.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/Omim/
The Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) database is a catalog of human genes and genetic disorders authored and edited by Dr. Victor A. McKusick and colleagues at Johns Hopkins and elsewhere, and developed for the World Wide Web by NCBI, the National Center for Biotechnology Information. The database contains textual information, pictures, and reference information. It also contains copious links to NCBI's Entrez database of MEDLINE articles and sequence information.

PiGBASE

PiGBASE, the genome database of the pig is maintained at The Roslin Institute, Edinburgh, Scotland. URL: http://www.ri.bbsrc.ac.uk/pigmap/pigbase/pigbase.html. (1995)

The North American node is maintained at the National Agricultural Laboratory of the USDA. URL: http://tetra.gig.usda.gov:8400/pigbase/manager.html

RATMAP

RATMAP is maintained at the Gothenburg University Lundberg Laboratory, Department of Genetics in Gothenburg, Sweden.
URL: http://ratmap.gen.gu.se

RATMAP is a database covering genes physically mapped to chromosomes in the laboratory rat. In addition to chromosomal localisation of genes and anonymous DNA sequences, RATMAP gives information about methods and accuracy of localisation (Mode and Status) and a short description of the genes. Appropriate literature references to each mapped rat gene are also given. Furthermore, RATMAP supports comparative mapping to several species. At present, comparative mapping data is given for bovine, human and the mouse.

SheepBase

SheepBase is maintained by an international editorial committee and held at AgResearch, Invermay Agricultural Centre, Dunedin, New Zealand.
URL: http://www.ri.bbsrc.ac.uk/cgi-bin/arkdb/browsers/browser.sh?species=sheep

SWISS-PROT

The SWISS-PROT Protein Sequence Data Bank, the European Bioinformatics Institute (EBI). URL: http://www.ebi.ac.uk/swissprot/

The SWISS-PROT Protein Sequence Data Bank is a database of protein sequences produced collaboratively by Amos Bairoch (University of Geneva) and EBI.

WashU

Genome Sequencing Center, Washington University School of Medicine St. Louis, MO. URL: http://genome.wustl.edu/est/mouse_esthmpg.html
The Genome Sequencing Center two-year project will produce expressed tag sequences (ESTs) from approximately 400,000 mouse cDNAs.
