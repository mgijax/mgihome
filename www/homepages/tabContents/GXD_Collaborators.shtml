Collaborators and Large-scale Data Providers:
  • EMAP (Edinburgh Mouse Atlas Project) and EMAGE (Edinburgh Mouse Atlas Gene Expression Database)

   GXD uses the Anatomical Dictionary of Mouse Development developed by EMAP to describe expression patterns in standardized ways.   GXD helps to refine the anatomical dictionary by providing continuous feedback.  GXD also provides its RNA in situ and Immunohistochemistry data to EMAGE, so that the data can be spatially mapped.  The ultimate goal of our collaboration is to provide the scientific community with integrated access to text-based and graphical expression data via the Mouse Gene Expression Information Resource.

  • GenePaint Database

   We have incorporated in situ hybridization data from GenePaint into GXD.  This data set can be viewed at J:122989.   Database entries in GXD link to the corresponding entries in GenePaint.  In this way, users can take advantage of GXD’s data integration and querying capabilities while having easy access to GenePaint’s high-resolution images and zoom capabilities.

  • Eurexpress

   We have incorporated RNA in situ hybridization data from Eurexpress into GXD. This data set can be viewed at J:153498.  Database entries in GXD link to the corresponding entries in Eurexpress. In this way, users can take advantage of GXD's data integration and querying capabilities while having easy access to Eurexpress's high-resolution images and zoom capabilities.

We have also incorporated data from the following major studies:

  • Shimogori et al. (2010) Nat Neurosci, 13, 767-775. They used in situ hypbridization to examine the expression of genes in the developing hypothalamus. This data can be viewed at J:157819.
  • Guo et al. (2010) Dev Cell 18, 675-685. They used real time PCR to study transcription factor dynamics in the preimplantation embryo. This data can be viewed at J:140465.
  • Gray et al. (2004) Science 306, 2255-2257.  They used in situ hybridization to examine the expression of putative transcription factors in the developing mouse nervous system.  This data can be viewed at J:91257.
  • Blackshaw et al. (2004) PLoS Biol 2, e247.  They used in situ hybridization to examine the expression of genes in the developing mouse retina.  This data can be viewed at J:93300.
  • Gitton et al. (2002) Nature 420, 586-590.  They used in situ hybridization and RT-PCR to examine the expression of human chromosome 21 orthologues in the mouse.  This data can be viewed at J:80501.
  • Reymond et al. (2002) Nature 420, 582-586.  They used in situ hybridization and RT- RT-PCR to examine the expression of human chromosome 21 orthologues in the mouse.  This data can be viewed at J:80502.
  • Freeman et al. (1998) MGI Direct Data Submission.  They used RT-PCR to examine the expression of genes in the mouse.  This data can be viewed at J:46439.
