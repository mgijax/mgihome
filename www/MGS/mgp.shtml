Introduction

In 1999, an international effort to sequence the mouse genome was started. The genome of the C57BL/6J inbred strain of mouse will be sequenced. The sequencing strategy involves targeted and systematic sequencing, and whole-genome shotgun approaches.

Project Goal

"Complete the sequence of the mouse genome by 2005. Mouse genomic sequence is an essential resource for interpreting human DNA sequence. For this reason, the centers sequencing human DNA are encouraged to devote up to 10% of their capacity to sequencing mouse DNA. Additional capacity for mouse DNA sequencing should be built up over the next few years with a goal of finishing the mouse sequence by 2005. Initially, a working draft of the mouse genome should be produced even sooner."

Collins, F., et al. New Goals for the U.S. Human Genome Project: 1998-2003. Science 282, 682-689 (1999) [full text]

BAC Library

The initial BAC clone library being used is RPCI-23 from Pieter de Jong's lab at Roswell Park Cancer Institute. The RPCI-23 library was constructed from kidney and brain tissue from a female C57BL/6J mouse. (170,634 clones, 192kb average insert size, with 11.2x coverage)
  • Library information from Roswell Park Cancer Institute
  • Cloning vector (Image of construct from RPCI) (GenBank: U80929)
  • Available from Research Genetics and Genome Systems
  • Mouse BAC-end Sequence Database at TIGR
The NCBI Clone Registry is used by sequencing groups to reserve clones.
