MGI announces 3.3 release

MGI is pleased to announce the release of Version 3.3 of the database. Version 3.3 integrates the data on mouse models of human disease and human disease etiology from OMIM with existing data for mouse genes and strains in MGI. This enhances the exploration of phenotypes, sequence, expression, orthology, and other related data. You can now use three of the MGI query forms (Phenotypes and Alleles, Genes and Markers, Mouse Sequence) to search for

  • existing or potential mouse models of a particular human disease
  • all human diseases that a particular mouse (genotype plus strain background) can be used to model.

Version 3.3 also incorporates data on mouse models sought without success, as well as models free from particular confounding diseases or phenotypic traits.

The database now links to Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) descriptions of human diseases, syndromes, and conditions. You can use the Human Disease Vocabulary Browser to browse the complete OMIM disease vocabulary and to search by disease name, accession ID, abbreviation, or synonym. If MGI has data associating a disease with a mouse model, that information appears in the browser and query results.

You can perform a query such as "Show me all the mouse models of diabetes that also exhibit insulin resistance" by entering, for example, diabetes and "insulin resistance" or 125853 and "insulin resistance" in the Phenotype/Disease box on any of the three query forms. (125853 is the OMIM accession ID for Diabetes Mellitus, Noninsulin-Dependent NIDDM.) The former search yields 36 alleles in 25 genes and the latter, 9 alleles in 9 genes in today's copy of the MGI database.

GXD adds data from two large-scale in situ hybridization screens

Gene Expression Database (GXD) is pleased to announce the inclusion of data from two major in situ hybridization studies.

Gray PA; Fu H; Luo P; Zhao Q; Yu J; Ferrari A; Tenzen T; Yuk DI; Tsung EF; Cai Z; Alberta JA; Cheng LP; Liu Y; Stenman JM; Valerious MT; Billings N; Kim HA; Greenberg ME; McMahon AP; Rowitch DH; Stiles CD; Ma Q. Mouse Brain Organization Revealed Through Direct Genome Scale TF Expression Analysis. 2004. Science 306:2255-2257.

Dr. Paul Gray and colleagues examined the expression of putative transcription factors in the developing mouse nervous system.

You can view the data set at accession number J:91257.

It includes:

  • 1332 genes
  • 1505 probes
  • 2766 assays (in situ hybridizations at E10.5, E13.5 and P0 stages)

Blackshaw S; Harpavat S; Trimarchi J; Cai L; Huang H; Kuo WP; Weber G; Lee K; Fraioli RE; Cho SH; Yung R; Asch E; Ohno-Machado L; Wong WH; Cepko CL. Genomic Analysis of Mouse Retinal Development. 2004. PloS Biol 2:E247.

Dr. Seth Blackshaw and colleagues examined the expression of genes in the developing retina.

You can view the data set at accession number J:93300.

It includes:

  • 1013 genes
  • 1025 probes
  • 1028 assays (in situ hybridizations at E12, E14, E16, E18, P0-1, P2-3, P4-5, P6-7, P8-10 and Adult stages)

Some useful links for querying subsets of data in GXD can be found here:

  • Gene Expression Data Query Form
  • Gene Expression Literature Index Query Form
  • Genes and Markers Query Form
  • Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form
If you are interested in submitting your data to GXD, please contact GXD@informatics.jax.org for further information.

Update to RIKEN clone data

The latest update to clone information in the MGI database includes all 102,801 large-insert cDNA clones generated by the RIKEN group in Japan. Details appear in a manuscript published in Science:

Carninci et al., The Transcriptional Landscape of the Mammalian Genome. 2005. Science 309:1559-1563

You can access the RIKEN clone information in MGI RIKEN or MGI clone identifiers, GenBank/DDBJ/EMBL accession identifiers (for the sequences associated with the clones), gene symbol or name, and MGI gene accession id. For a tab-delimited report of the RIKEN clone sequences and their current relationships to genes in MGI, see ftp://ftp.informatics.jax.org/pub/reports/MGI_RIKEN.rpt.

MGI is the community model organism database for the laboratory mouse.
