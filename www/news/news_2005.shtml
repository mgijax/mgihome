News and Announcements for 2005  

December 15, 2005
    New additions for MGI Version 3.41 include an electronic book, Origins of Inbred Mice, as well as performance enhancements, bug fixes, and updates.
November 30, 2005
    Deltagen and Lexicon Knockout Mice and MGI Phenotypic Data available
November 3, 2005
  • New features for MGI Version 3.4 include a Mouse SNP Query Form and a Protein Superfamily Vocabulary Browser.
  • MGI updates FANTOM3 GO annotations.
September 14, 2005
  • MGI Version 3.3 features the MGI Human Disease Vocabulary Browser, providing access to OMIM human disease terms for which Mendelian inheritance is suspected or known.
  • GXD adds data from two large-scale in situ hybridization screens.
  • MGI releases FANTOM3 Data.
August 12, 2005
The MGI genome coordinates for mouse genes are updated to Build 34. These coordinates come from computational annotations provided by Ensembl and NCBI. MGI's interactive sequence map, Mouse GBrowse, is also updated to Build 34 and now contains tracks indicating mouse genes associated with specific phenotypes. Access to Mouse GBrowse is from the MGI home page (www.informatics.jax.org) and from the sequence map section on MGI gene detail pages.
June 30, 2005
MGI is pleased to announce its 3.22 release.
June 7, 2005
Look for updates to the Gene Family pages and additions to the MGI Glossary.
May 26, 2005
For MGI Version 3.21, we've implemented Mouse GBrowse, a version of a generic genome browser, an electronic version of The Anatomy of the Laboratory Mouse by Margaret J. Cook, and a prototype SNP Query Form.

May 10, 2005
Version 3.2 of MGI features a completely redesigned web interface for accessing and interacting with phenotypic information. Mouse phenotypic data are integrated with mouse genes and strains in MGI, allowing exploration of sequence, expression, orthology, and other related data.
April 8, 2005
Version 3.12 of MGI adds a paragraph of summary information to the Gene Ontology (GO) Annotation Detail page, as well as performance enhancements and bug fixes.
February, 2005
Version 3.11 of MGI adds the ability to query by rat and human synonyms, a redesign of the MGI Glossary, and enhancements to performance.
