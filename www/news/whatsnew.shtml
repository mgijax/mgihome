July, 2004
  • International Mouse Strain Resource (IMSR) database update.
    The IMSR is a catalog of mouse strain availability at facilities around the world. Currently, IMSR contains strain data from JAX® Mice (JAX), the Mutant Mouse Regional Resource Centers (MMRRC), the Center for Animal Resources and Development (CARD) in Japan, and the Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) Mutant Mouse Database. Other sites will be added soon. Users can search for available strains by strain designation, strain status (e.g., live mice, cryopreserved embryos, sperm, etc.), mutations carried by a strain, and chromosome. The database provides strain holder information, links to information on specific strains and alleles, and links to the holder site for inquiries and order placement. We encourage participation of additional institutions and individuals who hold mice or cryopreserved embryos/gametes for distribution to participate by listing their holding in the IMSR.

  • MGI now links to Entrez Gene.
    The NCBI has introduced a new web resource, Entrez Gene, to replace Locus Link. Until the Locus Link resource is no longer available, MGI will provide links to both Entrez Gene and Locus Link web pages.

March, 2004
  • New Prototype Tool for in silico Positional Candidate Analysis
    The new     PositionaL candidate Analysis Display (PLAD) tool provides a list of genes and SNPs within a chromosomal interval defined by a mapped QTL or by a set of user-defined coordinates. For each gene in a genomic interval, links are provided to the MGI Gene Detail Report, a new Gene Ontology (GO) annotation viewer called VLAD, the UCSC Genome Browser, and a gene expression viewer (GEXview) at the Genomics Institute of the Novartis Research Institute. Both intergenic and genic SNPs are included in the report. For each SNP, links are provided to dbSNP and the UCSC Genome Browser. PLAD currently uses gene and SNP data related to Build 30 of the mouse genome sequence.

  • The Coat Colors of Mice by W. K. Silvers is available electronically as of March 8, 2004. This book, which has long been out of print, is a valuable resource for a wide range of biologists. Examples from coat colors illustrate many central concepts of genetics, such as an allelic series, gene interaction, and epistasis.
