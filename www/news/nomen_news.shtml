Allele and Mutant Data

October, 2000An Allele and New Mutant Submissions Form is available for contributing data to MGD. Information can be submitted for new or known alleles of existing genes or alleles where a phenotype has now identified a new gene. Help with nomenclature is provided from linked pages. Each new allele or mutant receives a permanent database accession ID. Mammalian Genome periodically publishes a list enumerating the data electronically submitted to MGD, including accession ID, chromosome, and locus and allele symbols, as a cross-referencing service for readers. For submission of other types of data to MGD, see the Data and Nomenclature Submissions page.

September, 2000: Nomenclature for the "Inhibitor of Apoptosis" Gene Family

The Mouse and Human Nomenclature Committees have approved a new nomenclature schema for the inhibitor of apoptosis gene family. The list of genes belonging to the inhibitor of apoptosis gene family can be found by querying the marker detail box using the approved family root symbol 'Birc', baculoviral IAP repeat-containing. Unfortunately, the stem symbol "Iap#" has already been approved for the intracisternal A particles assigned in 1980, and thus could not be used for the inhibitor of apoptosis family. The community decided to adopt the system used for the Saccharomyces cerevisiae genes: Birc#, baculoviral IAP repeat-containing.

The reference for members of this gene family is:

LaCasse EC, et al., Oncogene 1998 Dec 24;17(25):3247-59 PMID:9916987.

Revised Nomenclature for Two Families of Enzymes Hydrolyzing Extracellular Nucleotides

August, 2000: The nomenclature for two families of enzymes hydrolyzing extracellular nucleotides has been revised in mouse and human.

  1. Revised nomenclature for the Cd39 antigen family:
    The root symbol for this family is Entpd#, ectonucleoside triphosphate diphosphohydrolase #. For a complete listing of old and new symbols, enter the symbol Entpd in the Genes, Markers and Phenotypes Query Form.
  2. Revised nomenclature for the Pdnp, phosphodiesterase I/nucleotide pyrophosphatase family:
    The root symbol for this family is Enpp#, ectonucleotide pyrophosphatase/phosphodiesterase #. For a complete listing, enter the symbol Enpp in the Genes, Markers and Phenotypes Query Form.

The reference:

Zimmermann, H., Beaudoin, A.R., Bollen, M., Goding, J.W., Guidotti, G., Kirley, T.L., Robson, S.C. and Sano, K. (2000) Proposed nomenclature for two novel nucleotide hydrolyzing enzyme families expressed on the cell surface. Vanduffel, L. and Lemmens, R. (editors) Ecto-ATPases and related ectonucleotidases. Shaker Publishing B.V., Maastricht, p. 1-8.

July, 2000

Nomen News: Fatty Acid Binding Protein (FABP) Family Revised

The nomenclature for the fatty acid binding protein family has been revised to conform with the human nomenclature and what is currently in the literature. The root symbol for this family is Fabp# . For a complete listing of old and new symbols, enter the symbol Fabp or fatty acid binding protein in the Genes, Markers and Phenotypes Query Form.
