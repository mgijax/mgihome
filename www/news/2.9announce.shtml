The MGI 2.9 release features:

  • Toolbar

  • Marker detail page - tase of what's to come ...
  • New summary reports: sequence summary, gene ontology,m


New database links

  • Ensembl
    MGI now provides links to Ensembl mouse gene models from MGI markers that share a common external sequence accession ID with those models. We also obtain chromosome map locations for MGI genes associated with Ensembl gene models. Ensembl is a joint project between the European Bioinformatics Institute/European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL-EBI) and the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute.

  • DoTS/TIGR Mouse Gene Index
    MGI provides links to computationally derived transcript assemblies (DoTS and TIGR Mouse Gene Index) from MGI markers. Associations between MGI markers and assemblies are based on shared GenBank sequence accession identifiers. Only associations between MGI genes and TCs/DTs supported by non-conflicting evidence are accessible from web browsers for these resources. DoTS (Database Of Transcribed Sequences) is developed at CBIL (Computational Biology and Informatics Laboratory) at the University of Pennsylvania and is accessible at Allgenes web. TIGR Mouse Gene Index is developed at TIGR. Links are updated after each build of TIGR's Mouse Gene Index and CBIL's DoTS database or after every major change in MGI databases.
