MGI Release 2.9 Announcement - November 12, 2002  

The new features of MGI's 2.9 release are:

  • Ensembl

    MGI marker records are now linked to Ensembl mouse gene models that share a common external sequence accession ID with those models. We also obtain chromosome map locations for MGI genes associated with Ensembl gene models. Ensembl is a joint project between the European Bioinformatics Institute/European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL-EBI) and the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute.

  • CBIL/DoTS

    MGI marker records are also linked to computationally derived transcript assemblies from DoTS, the Database Of Transcribed Sequences, developed at the Computational Biology and Informatics Laboratory (CBIL) at the University of Pennsylvania. (See the AllGenes web site for more information). Associations between MGI markers and assemblies are based on shared GenBank sequence accession identifiers. Only associations between MGI genes and TCs/DTs supported by non-conflicting evidence are accessible for these resources. The links are updated after every major change in MGI databases.

  • TIGR Mouse Gene Index

    MGI marker records are linked as well to computationally derived transcript assemblies from the TIGR Mouse Gene Index developed at The Institute for Genomic Research (TIGR). Associations between MGI markers and assemblies are based on shared GenBank sequence accession identifiers. Only associations between MGI genes and TCs/DTs that are supported by non-conflicting evidence are accessible for these resources. The links are updated after each build of TIGR's Mouse Gene Index and after every major change in MGI databases.
