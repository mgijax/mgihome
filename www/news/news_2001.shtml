News and Announcements for 2001  

December, 2001


  • Mouse Genome Monthly Newsletter - December 24, 2001. Issue #2 of the newsletter is now online.
  • There are four new families in our Gene Family Information pages:
    1. Adenyl Cyclase
      Adenylate cyclases convert ATP to cAMP.
    2. Alpha Actinin
      Alpha actinins are actin-binding proteins.
    3. Fatty Acid Coenzyme A Ligase
      Members of the Facl# family convert free long-chain fatty acids into fatty acyl-CoA.
    4. Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid Ionotropic Type A (GABA-A) Receptor
      Gamma-aminobutyric acid ionotropic type A (GABA-A) receptors are ligand-gated chloride ion channels that mediate inhibitory synaptic transmission in the vertebrate central nervous system. These receptors are heterogenous pentamers of five types of subunits, alpha through delta and pi.

    November, 2001


  • Mouse Genetics by Lee M. Silver is available electronically as of November 28, 2001. This book, which has been out of print, is a valuable resource for all researchers in mouse genetics.
  • Collagen Gene Family - November 28, 2001. Collagen is the major extracellular structural protein in connective tissue and bone.
  • Mouse Genome Monthly Newsletter - November 13, 2001. Read about the latest progress from the Mouse Genome Sequencing Consortium.
  • Glypican Gene Family - November 13, 2001. The glypicans comprise a family of membrane-bound heparan sulphate proteoglycans. They have been implicated in cell growth and division and play a critical role in developmental morphogenesis.

    • , is a summary of the rules and guidelines for naming mouse genes and genomic sequences. There is a link to a query form so you can find your favorite symbol quickly, plus links to pages with more comprehensive material on the examples; there is also a link to the table of contents for the Mouse Nomenclature Rules and Guidelines.
