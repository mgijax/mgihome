Mouse Genome Informatics (MGI) Release 2.1 Announcement

November 17, 1998

The MGI Web site provides integrated access to various information resources on the genetics and biology of the laboratory mouse, including the Mouse Genome Database (MGD), the Gene Expression Database (GXD) and the Encyclopedia of the Mouse Genome (Encyclopedia).

Access the Mouse Tumor Biology Database

The Mouse Tumor Biology Database (MTB) prototype is a new resource accessible from the MGI home page. (Note: MTB is stored on the MGI home site (http://www.informatics.jax.org) so that links from the mirror sites will point to this location.) MTB includes information on tumors; tumor cell lines, pathology, incidence and latency data; genetic factors associated with tumors and tumor development and includes links to related information in MGD and the JAX Mice database.

This version of MTB is released as a prototype, not a fully developed database product. Comments and suggestions from the research community are welcome!

Access to Rat Data from MIT and MCW

The new Rat Data pages comprise a new resource stored on the MGI home site (http://www.informatics.jax.org). Links from the mirror sites will point to this location. The new resource presents data generated in the NIH-funded Rat Genome Project. These data come from two sources:

  • SSLP genetic mapping data generated from progeny of two different intercross matings (SHRSP x BN)F2 and (FHH x ACI)F2 by Dr. Eric Lander's group at the Whitehead Institute, MIT.

  • Rat polymorphism data generated by Dr. Howard Jacob's group at the Medical College of Wisconsin (MCW). 48 inbred strains were typed for PCR product length variation using the same primers used in the Whitehead/MIT SSLP mapping experiments.

Query forms provide access to information on rat SSLPs, polymorphisms, and genetic maps. This rat information constitutes a data set separate from MGD. The MGI website also provides access to rat homology information through MGD homology queries and rat strain descriptions through Michael Festing's listing of Inbred Strains of Mice and Rats

This version of the Rat Data resource is released as a prototype and is not a fully developed database product. Comments and suggestions from the research community are welcome!

Homology Links to Horse and Cat

MGD has included horse and cat homology information for some time. Now there are links from MGD homology records to Ark-Horse and Ark-Cat, databases included in ArkDB at the Roslin institute.

Link to FlyBase Mouse/Drosophila Homologies

The MGI Homology menu now provides a link to a display of just over 1000 Mouse/Drosophila homologies. The data are from a listing maintained by Michael Ashburner at FlyBase. The report shows MGI accession ID for the mouse gene (linked to the gene record in MGD), mouse gene symbol, MEDLINE ID for the reference (linked to the MEDLINE reference), FlyBase accession ID (linked to the FlyBase record) and the Drosophila gene symbol.

Other Enhancements

  • Updates to Michael Seldin's DNA mapping panel data set ((C3H/HeJ-Fasl<gld> x M. spretus)F1 x C3H/HeJ-Fasl<gld>) have been loaded into MGD.

  • A new schema browser provides access to schemas on all MGI servers enhanced searching options.

  • Updates to Michael Festing's listing of Inbred Strains of Mice and Rats have been loaded into MGD.

  • There have been minor improvements to detailed query results display for marker records and gene expression records.
