  • New members of gene families are being added rapidly. Mouse Genome Informatics Nomenclature Committee and HUGO are working with researchers to organize and update nomenclature on important gene family groups.

    • October, 2000

  • A new Allele and New Mutant Submissions Form is available for contributing data to MGD. Information can be submitted for new or known alleles of existing genes or alleles where a phenotype has now identified a new gene. Help with nomenclature is provided from linked pages. Each new allele or mutant receives a permanent database accession ID. Mammalian Genome periodically publishes a list enumerating the data electronically submitted to MGD, including accession ID, chromosome, and locus and allele symbols as a cross-referencing service for readers. For submission of other types of data to MGD, see the Data and Nomenclature Submissions page.

    February, 2000

  • NIH Sponsors the Targeted Sequencing of Mouse BAC clones. In undertaking the sequencing of the C57BL/6J mouse genome, sequencing centers are using a combined strategy of whole genome shotgun sequencing and targeted BAC clone sequencing. Applications are being accepted by NHGRI from researchers with interests in particular regions of biological significance to have BACs from those regions targeted for priority sequencing!

  • MGD and NCBI Joined Forces to Develop LocusLink for Mouse. MGD provides NCBI with daily updates of gene nomenclature (symbols and names), map positions, and the curated data that identifies GenBank accession ids with particular genes. NCBI uses these data to construct a new view of this information in the LocusLink interface and determines, for all sequences available for a particular gene, which they are designating as a reference sequence. (Note these NCBI reference sequences may be composite sequences and are not necessarily from C57BL/6J, the strain being used for the mouse genome sequencing project). LocusLink is available for human, mouse, and rat, and provides links back to the primary databases from which data were derived, so users can find additional information.
